# Upgrade Priority Guide

## Core Rule
**Depth before breadth.** Max out one mech and its primary weapon set before moving to a second mech.

## Stage 1: Early Game (Levels 1–15)

Focus:
1. Upgrade your starter mech's HP first — survivability buys time to learn
2. Upgrade primary weapon damage — DPS wins fights
3. Complete every daily mission for upgrade resources

Priority order:
- Mech HP → Weapon Damage → Mech Armor → Weapon Reload → Pilot upgrades

Avoid:
- Buying new mechs before your first is at least Level 10
- Splitting resources across multiple upgrade trees simultaneously

## Stage 2: Mid Game (Levels 15–30)

Focus:
1. Push your primary mech to maximum upgrade level
2. Begin upgrading a second mech that counters your primary's weaknesses
3. Invest in pilot abilities — they scale significantly with levels

Priority order:
- Primary Mech (max) → Second Mech HP → Pilot Level → Implants

## Stage 3: End Game (Levels 30+)

Focus:
1. Implant upgrades — they provide the highest percentage gains at this stage
2. Diversify into 3–4 mechs for situational picks
3. Optimize pilot/implant combinations for competitive modes

## Resource Management
- **A-Coins:** Use exclusively on core upgrades — never on cosmetics until endgame
- **Event tokens:** Save for legendary mech unlock events — best value in the game
- **Workshop items:** Craft pilot shards before mech shards — pilots unlock earlier benefits

## What NOT to upgrade
- Cosmetic skins and decals (zero gameplay impact)
- Mechs you never play in matches
- Weapons that don't fit your playstyle even if they are "meta"
